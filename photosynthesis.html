CO2 - O2 Cycle

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use energy from the sun to make glucose and oxygen.  Plants are also called producers because they produce their own food (the glucose) to sustain themselves.  There are four important ingredients necessary for photosynthesis.  Besides sunlight, plants use carbon dioxide, water, and chlorophyll.  The water enters the plants through its roots.  Sunlight and carbon dioxide enter the plant through its stomata.  Chlorophyll exists in the leaves and gives plants their green appearance.  The plant does not need the excess oxygen and releases it into the air as a byproduct.

Word

Definition

  • oxygen

a gas released by plants during photosynthesis that animals use for respiration

  • carbon dioxide

a gas produced by animals during respiration which plants use during photosynthesis

  • stomata

tiny holes in the leaves of plants which allows gases to enter and leave the plant

  • chlorophyll

a green substance in plant leaves that capture energy from the sun

  • leaf

a plant part that carries out photosynthesis to make food for the plant

  • stem

a plant part that holds leaves up and moves water and nutrients throughout the plant

  • roots

plant parts that hold the plant in place and take in water and nutrients

Photosynthesis is just one part of another larger cycle called the carbon dioxide-oxygen cycle.  The carbon dioxide-oxygen cycle is the movement of carbon dioxide and oxygen between organisms and the air.  During photosynthesis, plants release oxygen into the atmosphere.  Animals release carbon dioxide during respiration.  This is very important for organisms to survive.  Without oxygen, animals cannot breathe and survive.  Without carbon dioxide, plants cannot make their own food through photosynthesis.  Plants and animals depend on each other to survive.
