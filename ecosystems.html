All About Ecosystems

  •    Ecosystems Defined

    Organisms live all over the world in different ecosystems.  Organisms need certain things to survive such as food and air.  They find what they need in their environments by interacting with other living organisms and nonliving things in their environment.  Ecosystems are all the populations of living organisms and the nonliving components in an environment, as well as the interactions among them.

  •    Biotic Factors

    Biotic factors are the living organisms in an ecosystem.  They are the plants and animals which require energy to survive, and they have basic needs which must be met.  These organisms serve different roles in the ecosystem, such as:

     

    • producers
    • consumers
    • decomposers
    • predators
    • prey

  •    Abiotic Factors

    Abiotic factors are the nonliving components in an ecosystem.  These help the biotic factors to survive.  The living organisms need these to meet their needs, and without them the living organisms would die.  They in include:

     

    • heat
    • light
    • air
    • water
    • soil and rocks
