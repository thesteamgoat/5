Types of Organisms

There are many different kinds of organisms, and they are all important parts in a food chain.  Starting at the top of any food chain is the sun.  The sun provides energy for producers, which are plants and algae and some bacteria.  Producers are organisms that make their own food.  Producers capture energy from the sun in order to make their own food through photosynthesis.  A producer always makes up the first link in a food chain.

The opposite of a producer is a consumer.  A consumer makes up the rest of the links in a food chain.  Consumers are animals that get their energy from eating other organisms.  When a consumer eats a producer, it gets the energy that the producer received from the sun.  The first consumer in a food chain is called a primary consumer.  There are different kinds of consumers:  herbivores, carnivores, omnivores, and decomposers.

Herbivores are one kind of consumer.  Herbivores are animals that get their energy by eating only plants.  Examples of herbivores are rabbits, deer, horses, buffalo, and cows.  Many herbivores are called grazers because they must spend most of their time grazing to consume enough energy.  Some humans choose to be herbivores because they do not want to eat animal products for various reasons.  These humans are called vegans.

Carnivores are another type of consumer.  Carnivores are animals that get their energy by eating only other animals.  Lions, tigers, sharks, wolves, foxes, and crocodiles are all examples of carnivores.  Carnivores are often called predators, and the animals they eat are their prey.  Many kinds of birds are carnivorous, such as hawks, eagles, and owls.  For this reason, these predatory birds are called "birds of prey."

Some consumers eat both plants and animals.  These consumers are called omnivores.  Omnivores are animals that get their energy by eating both plants and animals.  Pigs, skunks, raccoons, squirrels, turtles, and most humans are examples of omnivores.  Some bears like the polar bear are carnivores, but a most are omnivores.  For example, a grizzly bear will eat both fish and berries.

Decomposers are a special type of consumer.  Decomposers are organisms that get their energy by feeding on dead plants and animals, as well as animal wastes.  Bacteria and fungi, such as mushrooms, are decomposers.  When living organisms die, decomposers break down the dead material and return it to the soil  They get energy from the dead material and provide nutrients for other organisms to use.
