Deserts are very dry places with little precipitation.  Very few plants and animals can live in the harsh conditions, and those that do have special adaptations.  Most people think of deserts as hot places with lots of sand, but some deserts are cold with rocks and ice instead.  An example is Antarctica which is the largest desert on Earth.  A hot, dry desert may have lots of cactus plants, mesquite trees, reptiles, and some birds.

Tropical rain forests have a warm and humid climate.  The conditions make rain occur frequently, sometimes as often as daily.  Many trees and other plants grow there such as ferns and flowering bushes.  The trees can grow so tall and thick that sunlight does not reach the ground; this is called the canopy.  Many types of animals live in the trees and on the forest floor, including gorillas, monkeys, parrots, frogs, jaguars, and insects.

The grasslands are large areas of mostly flat land covered with tall grasses but few trees.  The climate is warm to hot and dry, but not as dry as a desert.  Herbs and short shrubs grow there.  The African grasslands are called savannas and are home to lions, zebras, snakes, and elephants.  The grasslands in North America are sometimes called prairies and are home to bison, deer, skunks, mice, badgers, and hawks.

Deciduous forests have four seasons.  Summers are warm, and winters are cold.  Springs and autumns are mild.  Maple, oak, and elm trees grow there, along with other types of plants.  Most of the trees lose their leaves in autumn.  The animals that live there include bears, foxes, beavers, wolves, raccoons, deer, and squirrels.  Many birds live there including eagles, hawks, and owls.

Taigas have long, cold winters and short, mild summers.  Mostly coniferous trees grow there, such as spruce, fir, and pine.  Taigas reach as far north as the tree line, which is where trees stop growing because the climate is too harsh.  Mosses and lichens are common.  Plenty of animals brave the cold, including bears, foxes, moose, elk, owls, eagles, and many insects.  Sometimes these are called coniferous forests.

The tundra is a place with flat, open ground that is always frozen.  It is cold there all year long.  Some grasses grow in the tundra, but few types of plants live there because of the harsh climate.  Shrubs, mosses, and lichens also grow there.  The climate is too harsh and the growing season too short for trees.  Some animals that live there include musk ox, caribou, polar bears, Arctic hares, Arctic fox, and snowy owls.
